02

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device — computer, tablet, or smartphone — when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide useful information to website owners.

Cookies do not contain personal information like your name or email address on their own. They typically store an anonymous identifier or preference setting that helps the website function better for you during and between visits.

ℹ️
Session vs. Persistent Cookies Session cookies are temporary — they are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device until they expire or you delete them manually. Both types are commonly used across most modern websites.

Analytics Cookies

Used by services like Google Analytics to collect anonymous data about how visitors use the website — which pages are visited, how long users stay, and where traffic comes from. All data is aggregated and anonymized.

Managing Cookies

You have full control over cookies through your browser settings. You can choose to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or delete existing cookies stored on your device at any time.

Most browsers also allow you to set preferences for specific websites, so you can block cookies from third-party sites while allowing cookies from websites you trust.

⚠️
Impact of Disabling Cookies Disabling analytics or advertising cookies will not stop you from using the website, but you may see less relevant ads. Disabling essential cookies may prevent certain features — such as contact forms — from working correctly.
07

Browser Cookie Controls

You can manage or delete cookies directly through your browser settings. Below are step-by-step paths for the most common browsers:

🌐 Google Chrome
Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
🦊 Mozilla Firefox
Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Manage Data
🧭 Apple Safari
Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
🔵 Microsoft Edge
Settings → Cookies and Site Permissions → Manage and Delete Cookies and Site Data

For mobile browsers, cookie settings are typically found under your browser's privacy or site settings in the app menu.
